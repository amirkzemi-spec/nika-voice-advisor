Your bank statements must show that the full amount of money that you need has been in the account for 28 consecutive days up to the date of the closing balance.
This means that the account must not have dropped below the amount that you need to show at any time during the 28-day period.
If it does, your immigration application is likely to be refused.
In addition, the final date of this 28-day period must not be more than 31 days old on the date that you submit your application.
Maintenance must be in the form of cash funds and immediately accessible.
You cannot use evidence of other types of finances, such as shares, bonds, or a pension fund.
